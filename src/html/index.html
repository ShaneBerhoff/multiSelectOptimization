Multi-Select Optimization

Solves for the expected amount of points you would get for a question based on a certain amount of options, correct answers, and options guessed.

Options: 5

Correct: 1

Guesses: 1


Solves the expected points for all possible combinations of number of correct answers and options guessed. Used to determine the number of guesses with the highest expected value in points.

Options: 5


Equal probability of all 1-N correct answers.

The selected number of correct answers has a probability of 1 and the rest have a probability of 0.

Since the possible number of correct answers is discrete this approximates a normal distrabution about a mean μ and a standard deviation σ for the values.

Input a custom probability distribution where the position of the probability coresponds to the number of correct answers it applies to. For example .3,.4,.3 sets the probability of 1 correct answer to .3, probability of 2 correct answers to .4, and the probability of 3 correct to .3.
